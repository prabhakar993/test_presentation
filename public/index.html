Literature survey : Cavitation bubble dynamics

Author Contribution
Rayleigh et al. (1917) Considered the growth and collapse of a spherical bubble in an infinite fluid and showed that tremendous dynamic pressures arise during the collapse phase of the bubbleâ€™s life.
Benjamin et al. (1966) Recorded the asymmetric collapse of an initially spherical vapour cavity near a rigid boundary and observed that the bubble collapses with a high-speed liquid jet directed towards the fluid-structure interface.
Gibson et al. (1968) Conducted experiments near flexible boundaries and suggested that the physical properties of the boundary determined the direction of movement of the liquid jet during the cavitation bubble collapse phase.
Plesset et al. (1971) Predicted Benjamin and Ellis observations theoretically with help of finite-difference solution. These predictions are experimentally confirmed by Lauterborn and Bolle (1975).
Chahine et al. (1977) Carried out a theoretical and experimental study on the growth and collapse of a cavitation bubble near a free surface and suggested that when the cavitation bubble collapses at a sufficient distance away from the free surface, the free surface also grows and collapses with the bubble growth and collapse.
